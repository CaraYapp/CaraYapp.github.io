Home Sweet Home

November 19, 2009 Cara

You’ll be pleased to know that this post is written:

a) at 1pm, not stupid o’clock in the morning!

b) I’m not feeling high or low on drugs!

c) I’m at home!

After the weekend which was busy sorting things out around the house etc. I went back into Frimley Park Hospital on Monday for a planned blood count check.  As you’ll know, usually I end up having to stay in, having gone neutropenic and needing IV antibiotics everytime apart from last time, and needing blood transfusions and platelets every time.  This time I was feeling much better and had a good feeling about it…I was still exhausted but in a different way and more because I’d been busy and not sleeping rather than just being exhausted from the drugs.  Anyway, I was optimistic and so took less stuff with me (less than the usual car full of pinkness I take!) but I still had to go prepared to stay in so had a few days worth of pj’s etc.  So Monday was spent in my own room in the private suite, lunch and dinner enjoyed there (poor Goose watching me eat it!) and it got to the evening and we’d still had no results.  So my sister came in to visit me and Chris was on his way to visit when I was told my blood count was fine and I could go home!  So at about 8.30pm I left!  Chris literally arrived just after I was told so in fact picked me up rather than visited!  It was very bizarre spending the day and evening in the room and then leaving!  Luckily I hadn’t changed into my pjs as I was feeling better than normal and hopeful that I might be discharged.  So I’ve been at home all week!  No blood transfusions, no platelets, no drips, no being woken at stupid o’clock, just home sweet home!  It’s been lovely to spend time here and to do some of the things I like to do and just to relax.  I’m still having to take it easy as it’s still my vulnerable time and I’ve got to go back tomorrow for another blood count check just in case.  But hopefully that’ll be fine and I’m free until 30th November when chemo 10 is due…apart from 27th November which is another trip to Oxford to see Prof and discuss my progress.

So all in all good news.  I’m feeling better than I usually do and I’m at home.  The steroids are finished for a few weeks so I’m more back to normal.  I’m making it sound easy  now but it’s still far from it.  I’m generally happy and cheerful and just getting on with it, taking each day as it comes but also planning for the days ahead so I can make the most of them.  Yet I’ve still managed to have some outbursts of tears when the floodgates have opened and I just can’t stopped…I can’t tell you why, there’s not just one reason.  It’s just cancer and everything that comes with it and sometimes it just hits me all over again.  Chris usually has the pleasure of this as it’s usually at night when I lie down to go to sleep when everything comes into my head.  But all girls will know that sometimes you just need a good cry and lots of cuddles and everything seems better after that.  & so that’s what I did!

Right, well that’s all for now.  If you’re on facebook, have a look at my latest pics and the video I’ve put on my wall.   You will LAUGH!  I’ve sent it into You’ve Been Framed, I hope it gets on!  I’ll try to post it on here soon if I can work it out!

Thanks again for all your messages and comments, more and more of you are coming out of the woodwork!  Like I’ve said before, I love finding out who’s reading and I’ve got back in touch with so many people from years ago because of it, which I love!  So don’t be shy!
