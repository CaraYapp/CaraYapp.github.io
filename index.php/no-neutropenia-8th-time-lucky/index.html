No neutropenia! 8th time lucky!

October 30, 2009 Cara

I’ve now made it to Friday without going neutropenic!  It’s a miracle!  I went into Frimley Park Hospital on Monday for a blood check and as predicted I did have to stay in.  However, it was progress as I wasn’t neutropenic, I was just (I say just!) anaemic and needed a  unit blood transfusion and platelets.  So I stayed in and had that on Tuesday and was out by Wednesday lunchtime!  Today I’ve been back for another blood count check just in case but I haven’t heard anything so I’m assuming everything’s ok, otherwise I’d be whizzed back in and put on a drip and antibiotics faster than I could say “but…!”

So, this is what I wanted and means I can continue chemo on the reduced dose.  Mixed feelings are brewing however…I’d kind of got use to the idea of having to stop and although this scared me due to the prospects of the cancer coming back if I hadn’t completed the course, I’d started to get secretly excited about not having to go through the chemo anymore and the thought of being a step closer to getting my life back… However I have to focus on why I’m going through it and I’m not getting this far to stop too soon so I will continue on my ‘journey’…I have the option to stop at anytime now if it’s too much  for me but no matter how tempting it is I WILL NOT BE DEFEATED by this!  I’m telling myself that as much as I’m telling you!

Anyway I’ve managed to fit in lots of visits this week…mainly due to it being half-term and so I’ve seen lots of my teacher friends!  On Tuesday my friend Vicky came to see me.  We did teacher training together and were very close but now she’s living back in Jersey (on the rock as she calls it!) where she’s from so it’s been over 2 years since I’d seen her!  Unfortunately I was in hospital for her visit, but it was still lovely to catch up after so long.  Then on Wednesday I left hospital by 12 and had already managed to plan a visit from Katie and Allie who I use to work with at Frogmore.  So they came round to see my house and we went out for a lovely lunch and chatted and gossiped until we realised it was 6pm!  Oops!  On Thursday my friend J came to visit, who I also did teacher training with.  We lived together in Southampton after we qualified too.  She’s now living in Colchester so we don’t see each other as much as we’d like but she’s been brilliant at sending me things to make me smile since I’ve been ill and she’s been to visit quite a few times too.

S0 I’ve been a lucky girl this week with visits!  Today I had to go to hospital (yawn!) for blood tests and for physio (not so yawn!)  A young fit male stroking my leg…and my bum today…!  It’s part of the exercises!!  IT IS!!  The good news is I’m now allowed to walk without wearing the leg splint that I’ve had to strap onto me for the past 7 weeks.  It feels weird, weak and wobbly but it’s progress so I’m happy.

I’m in a funny mood today, a bit frustrated by the limitations I have on me…even when I’ve recovered from the worst effects of the chemo I’m still so dependant on other people to help me because of my leg.  The worst thing about being on crutches is not being able to carry anything!  & that it hurts your hands…& that they fall over everytime you try to prop them up…ok ok so there’s quite a few annoying things about being on crutches but I won’t go on!  I still remind myself constantly that I’m lucky to have my leg and lucky to be here.  Maybe I’m not so lucky having got cancer in the first place but I can’t dwell on that and must  focus on the positives…

My dinner’s ready now, prepared by Jamie Oliver AKA Chris Newton!   I’ll write again soon and I think I need to jazz this site up with some photos so I’ll do my best…

Ciao for now! xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
